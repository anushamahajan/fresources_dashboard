# Fresources

> The one-stop study platform every Indian engineering student needed but nobody had built.

**[Analytics Dashboard](https://anushamahajan.github.io/fresources_dashboard/)** · **[Case Study](https://drive.google.com/file/d/17g6s9BF-D3fnHpCXQIF-irbZ4WDuVprt/view?usp=sharing)**

---

## The Problem

Study resources in Indian engineering colleges exist in fragments. Notes are with one person, PYQs are in a random Telegram group, the right YouTube playlist takes hours to find. But the problem runs deeper than just scattered files.

Notes in engineering colleges are **teacher-specific**. The paper comes from what your professor taught, not a generic syllabus. A student in ECE sem 3 at DTU under one professor needs completely different notes than someone studying the same subject at NSUT. Generic resource sites couldn't solve this.

We saw batchmates failing not from lack of effort, but lack of access. So we decided to fix the infrastructure.

---

## What We Built

A structured, searchable, constantly-updated study resource platform organised by **college → department → year → semester → subject → resource category**. Every resource mapped to a specific teacher's context. A student in Mech sem 4 at NSUT sees only what's relevant to them, nothing else.

Launched at DTU. Expanded to IGDTUW, NSUT, IIIT, and IP University.

---

## Features

| Feature | What it solved |
|---|---|
| **Granular resource taxonomy** | College, branch, year, sem, subject, teacher-level organisation. No more irrelevant results. |
| **Search and filters** | Subject-level filters across all colleges and branches. Navigating to the right content went from multiple clicks to seconds. |
| **Embedded YouTube player** | Removed the download button to keep engagement on-platform. Embedded YouTube iframes directly into subject pages so students watch, read, and save without leaving the site. |
| **Favourites / star** | Students save resources for quick access during exam prep. |
| **Session resume** | Platform saves each user's last active college, year, semester, and subject. Resumes from that checkpoint on the next visit. |
| **Admin dashboard with audit logs** | Volunteers can create everything: years, semesters, subjects, categories, resources. Full observability into every upload and deletion. Role-based permissions tiered by community position. |
| **PYQ section** | Dedicated section for previous year questions. Students Googled for PYQs and landed on us — turned search intent into retention. |
| **HackMate** | Identified a second problem: students couldn't find hackathon teammates. Built skill-based matching and chat inside Fresources. |

**Exam season pattern** — traffic spiked every September, October, November, December, February, March, May, and June without fail. Midsem and endsem seasons across both semesters, every year, compounding. The growth was structural, not viral.

---

## How It Grew

**Volunteer network** — 150 to 200 volunteers across 5 colleges, 70 to 100 consistently active. Each volunteer owned their branch: all uploads, all students in their department depending on them.

**WhatsApp distribution** — groups mirroring the college hierarchy: one per college, one per branch inside each college. Every upload pushed immediately to the right group.

**Instagram** — 2,500 followers with student-made content: tips, threads, senior experiences. Peer-to-peer, not branded.

**Word of mouth** — Fresources was prominent enough that by exam season, students opened it directly without needing a reminder. 60% of lifetime sessions came through direct traffic.

---

## The Call We Made

We explored monetisation: paid PYQs, exclusive content, mentorship. Hosting costs for a multi-college database were real, plagiarism risk on paid PDFs was uncontrollable, and charging students a fee for something built to help them didn't feel right.

We kept it a community platform. Knowing when not to monetise is a product decision too.
